## 5. Why you want to join Amber

I'm a backend engineer by training, not a designer, but I've spent the last several months building frontend products where I had to make real design decisions without a Figma file to follow, and somewhere in that process I realised I care deeply about how things feel to use, not just whether they work. What pulls me to Amber specifically is the user: an international student trying to find housing in a city they've never been to, probably stressed, possibly in a completely different timezone, is one of the harder emotional design problems to solve well. The stakes are real, the trust has to be earned fast, and Amber is now layering AI on top of that already delicate user journey across 80 million students and 140 cities. That intersection of a high-stakes human problem and an AI product layer is exactly the kind of thing I want to be thinking about at the start of my career. I'd rather be honest that I'm coming from engineering and bring that perspective genuinely than oversell a design background I don't have.
