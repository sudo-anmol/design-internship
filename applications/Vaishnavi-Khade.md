## 4. A project you've shipped recently

https://www.behance.net/gallery/248350139/Lympha-Lipdema-Project-Case-Study

This is my recent project that I made for my inclusive design class, where I designed a daily management app for lipedema patients. 
It is a condition where abnormal amounts of fat get stored in different parts of the body, and many people unfortunately mistake it 
for obesity.
I conducted both primary and secondary research to understand the condition and user needs better, and then built out a feature list 
supported by 5 task flows and end to end UI screens. The app helps users manage their symptoms without having to rely on generic tools
that do not really understand what they are going through.
One thing I would improve is adding more gamification while users input their responses. I focused a lot on asking the right questions 
to get meaningful insights, but adding gamified elements would make the process more engaging and less daunting. That is something I 
definitely want to work on going forward.

## 5. Why you want to join Amber

When I moved to a new city for college, I had the safety of a hostel until my final year, when I had to find housing on my own, 
and even then the uncertainty of not knowing where you will live, whether the place is legit, or if you are being overcharged was 
genuinely overwhelming. A student stepping off a plane in a country they have never lived in faces all of that, plus the language, 
the culture, the timezone, and the loneliness all at once. That scale of daunting is exactly why having a platform that is actually 
in your corner matters so much, and it is what makes Amber’s problem space feel important to me. What I like about Amber is that it 
does not stop at solving accommodation, it understands that a new student is trying to get their footing in every direction. 
Bundling in things like eSIM discounts, flight discounts, and language lessons offers shows a team that asked what else this student 
is scared of and actually went and solved those things too.
