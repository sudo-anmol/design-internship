## 4. A project you've shipped recently

Hospital Dietetics Delivery System — a safety-critical healthcare platform.

Nursing staff were making meal delivery errors because critical constraints (diet restrictions, patient condition, timing) were not visible at the point of decision. I redesigned the system around real-time constraint awareness by introducing a 3-layer validation model (Safe / Flagged / Blocked) that surfaces risk instantly.

I conducted user interviews, mapped decision flows, and designed interaction patterns where the system prevents unsafe actions instead of relying on user memory. In usability testing, the final system achieved zero critical errors and reduced decision time by ~40%.

What I’d change now:
I would extend this system with an AI layer that explains why something is risky or blocked, not just enforce rules — shifting from validation to decision support.

## 5. Why you want to join Amber

The interesting part of Amber isn’t listing student housing — it’s helping users make a high-stakes decision in an unfamiliar environment.

From what I understand, the core problem is not lack of options, but lack of clarity. Students are comparing tradeoffs across price, location, safety, and trust signals — often without enough context to decide confidently.

That’s the kind of problem I’ve worked on before: systems where the cost of a wrong decision is high, and the interface needs to guide users without overwhelming them.

I’m particularly interested in exploring how AI can reduce decision anxiety — not by adding more information, but by structuring it, explaining tradeoffs, and making outcomes more predictable.

Amber feels like a place where design, engineering, and AI need to come together to solve a messy, real-world problem at scale — that’s exactly the kind of work I want to do.
