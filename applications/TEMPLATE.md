## 4. A project you've shipped recently

The problem statement: 
Goodreads has 150M+ users but people actively avoid it, I wanted to understand why.

What I found:
* Non-readers aren't uninterested in books — Goodreads just makes starting feel overwhelming
* People discover books on Instagram and Pinterest, and only land on Goodreads to read reviews, discovery is completely broken
* Readers are used to current social media habits, i.e., scrolling, taste based feed section. Genres are outdates, vibes is the new wave.

What I did: 
Redesigned around that core insight. Introduced emotion-driven Book Bundles curated by AI sentiment extraction, a social Reader Moments layer, and quote cards that nudge curiosity the way Netflix trailers do.

What I'd change: 
The "I'm Confused" mode — a 3-question flow for lost readers. In hindsight, mood is not a clean input. Real reading decisions are messier than a form can capture. I'd rebuild it as a conversation, not a questionnaire.

## 5. Why you want to join Amber
Student housing is a problem I understand from the inside because I’ve been through it myself. I’ve dealt with sketchy listings, hidden costs, confusing brokerage, and landlords with rigid rules, all while trying to figure things out in a city I hadn’t even seen yet. But that’s not the only reason I want to work at Amber. What draws me more is how Amber is choosing to solve this problem.

Most design internships look for someone to push pixels. But what I love about Amber is how it is looking for someone who doesn’t box themselves into “designer” or “developer,” but instead thinks like a problem solver. The kind of perfection-enthusiast that I am, it is difficlut for me to just be on one side, and not think about the other side, because it is the whole product that I care about, and it is so rare for a company to acknowledge it. I am grateful for Amber that it is trying to dimish that gap between the roles and and even this GitHub-based resume submission reflects that philosophy, something I absolutely loved.

I’m someone who doesn’t just use AI, I understand it at a fundamental level. From regression and classification to neural networks, backpropagation and CNNs, I’ve worked on projects where I’ve had to think deeply about how models behave, where they fail, and how they can be improved. So when I look at a problem, I don’t just see features, I see systems.

If a problem is a jar, I don’t just see it from the outside, I can see through it. I understand what’s happening inside, how the data flows, how decisions are being made and at the same time, I can step outside and understand how that entire system feels to the user, and I can design better experiences for them without disrupting what’s already working inside. That dual perspective is what I bring to the table.

For a platform like Amber, the core challenge isn’t just discovery, it’s trust at scale, helping someone confidently book the right place in a city they’ve never been to. Amber is at an interesting inflection point, scaling toward $1B in annual gross bookings while rethinking how AI integrates into the product. Solving discovery and trust in student housing isn’t just a logistics problem, it’s fundamentally a design and AI problem, and I am confident I can be a great asset in building that solution.
