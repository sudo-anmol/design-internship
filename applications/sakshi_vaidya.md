## 4. A project you've shipped recently

_One project I worked on was improving key user flows in Snapmint’s checkout and onboarding experience.

The core problem was around drop-offs and hesitation during decision-making especially in a fintech context where users need clarity and trust before committing. Snapmint’s goal itself is to make purchases accessible through simple EMI options without credit cards , so reducing friction in these flows was critical.

I worked on analyzing user behavior and identifying where users were getting confused or dropping off. Based on that, I redesigned parts of the flow by:
simplifying steps and reducing cognitive load
improving information hierarchy (especially around pricing/EMI clarity)
making decision points more guided instead of overwhelming

The outcome was a more intuitive and confidence-driven flow, where users could move forward without second-guessing.

This project helped me understand how small UX changes—especially in high-trust flows like payments can directly impact conversion and user confidence._
