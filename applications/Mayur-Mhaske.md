## 5. Why you want to join Amber

What pulled me in wasn't the design brief — it was the problem. International students are making one of the biggest decisions of their lives (where they'll live, alone, in a foreign city) and doing it through a booking flow that still feels transactional. I've built products for real users with real constraints — a warehouse system for farmers in Marathi, an AI tool that removes friction from a developer's workflow — and the pattern I keep coming back to is: when the stakes are high for the user, the design has to do more than look good, it has to actually reduce anxiety. Amber is working exactly in that gap. The AI Product Design angle is specifically why I applied here over other internships — I already build with AI (Gemini API, generative UI), and I want to learn how to make AI decisions feel trustworthy to a user who's scared of making the wrong choice. That's a harder design problem than most, and I want to be in the room where it's being solved.
