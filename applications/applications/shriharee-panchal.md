## 4. A project you've shipped recently

**Agentic RAG Chatbot** — github.com/shrihareepanchal

Built an end-to-end Agentic RAG system using LangGraph 
and ChromaDB that handles 6 file formats, routes queries 
across 4 reasoning steps, and responds in under 2 seconds 
on a 20K-document corpus. I designed the entire 
interaction model — how the agent decides what tool to 
call, how it communicates uncertainty to the user, and 
how guardrails surface without breaking the conversation 
flow. If I were to redo it, I'd invest more in the 
failure states — what the system shows when it genuinely 
doesn't know, rather than confidently returning a 
low-confidence answer. That's the hardest design problem 
in agentic interfaces and I only partially solved it.

## 5. Why you want to join Amber

Student housing is one of those problem spaces that looks 
simple from the outside — find a place, pay rent, move 
in — and reveals itself as genuinely complex the moment 
you're inside it. Payments across currencies, compliance 
across cities, universities with incompatible systems, 
students who are stressed and time-poor. I've been that 
student. I know what it feels like when a platform adds 
friction instead of removing it.

What pulls me to Amber specifically is the AI-native 
design philosophy. I already work this way — I use Claude 
and Cursor daily, not as shortcuts but as thinking 
partners. I've built agentic systems and LLM interfaces 
from scratch, which means I understand where AI actually 
helps users and where it gets in the way. Most designers 
working on AI products haven't built one. I have.

The "curious beyond screens" line in your JD is what made 
me stop scrolling. Voice interfaces, ambient UI, agents 
that act without being prompted — these are the surfaces 
I want to design for. Not because it's trendy, but 
because I genuinely believe the next decade of product 
design lives there, and I want to build it.
