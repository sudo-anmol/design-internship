## 4. A project you've shipped recently
There was a drop-off in the onboarding flow of the company’s B2C intern platform, where users had to complete every profile section before submission intended to ensure better profiles and improve interview chances, but it led to frustration. We addressed this by making some sections optional while keeping key ones required, adding contextual prompts like “complete this to boost your profile,” and introducing a submit-time popup that highlighted incomplete sections and framed a “5-star profile” as ideal, while still allowing users to submit anyway.


## 5. Why you want to join Amber
Amber stands out to me because it works on a problem that actually shapes a student’s future. Choosing to study abroad isn’t a simple decision it’s confusing, high-stakes, and often overwhelming, and I like working on problems where design can genuinely reduce that stress. From my experience designing for students, especially in onboarding and decision-heavy flows, I’ve seen how small changes can improve clarity and confidence. I feel I’d be a strong fit because I naturally think in terms of simplifying complex journeys and guiding users step by step. Being able to do that in a space that directly impacts students’ lives is what really pulls me towards Amber.
