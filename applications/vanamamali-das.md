## 5. Why you want to join Amber

I have a stable job, Senior Presentation Associate at Williams Lea, 5 years in, decent pay, and I'm choosing to step back and do an internship. That 
probably tells you more about my intent than anything else I could write here.

What pulls me to Amber specifically is the intersection of a real, high-stakes user problem and AI. Finding housing abroad as a student is genuinely 
stressful, you're in a new country, you don't know who to trust, the stakes are high and the information is scattered. That's exactly what happened to
me when I moved out of my house, well I didn't change countries but I did change states, so I understand the problem as I have faced it on a
different scale, this is the kind of messy, human problem I want to be designing for. Getting to design for that problem, at that scale, would be
the most meaningful thing I've done in my career so far. The fact that Amber is leaning into AI to solve it makes it even more exciting to me.
I'm deeply fascinated by what AI-assisted products can do when the design is intentional and human-centered, not just bolted on.

My background is in translating complex information into clear, decisions. I've done it for financial and consulting data for years. I think that 
skill transfers directly to what Amber needs: helping students make one of the biggest decisions of their life feel manageable. I want to be in a room 
where that work is happening.

For the past 1.5 years, I have been working on UX after 9 hours of job, I would come home to work 6-7 hours on my projects, while living away
from home and manage day to day activities, I know I have a far way to go from here but I have never stopped learning, and this opportunity feels like
I could learn alot.
