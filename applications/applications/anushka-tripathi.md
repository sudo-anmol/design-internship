## 4. A project you've shipped recently

[_Github Repo (WIP)_](https://github.com/Anushka644/spare-adaptive-save-engine.git)

Spare — Fintech Savings & Investment App

Spare is a passion project I’ve been building to explore how better financial product design can help users develop stronger saving and investing habits through automated workflows and clearer financial decision-making.

I led the product thinking and UX end-to-end, from identifying user pain points through secondary research (including building a Python-based Reddit scraper to analyze real finance communities) to defining user flows, feature priorities, and simplifying transaction journeys for better usability.

What I’d change now: I’d spend more time validating assumptions through direct user interviews earlier instead of relying heavily on secondary research first. It would have helped sharpen feature prioritization faster and reduce unnecessary solution exploration.

## 5. Why you want to join Amber

Amber sits at an interesting intersection of trust, decision-making, and high-stakes user behavior. Students moving abroad are not just booking housing, they’re making one of the most stressful financial and life decisions they’ll face, often with limited information and high anxiety.

That kind of problem space is far more interesting to me than polished but low-impact design work. I’m drawn to products where better UX can directly reduce uncertainty and improve real outcomes for users.

I also like that this role expects product thinking, not just screen design. The pull request application itself made that clear, it signals ownership, clarity, and builder mindset over titles. That’s the kind of team I want to learn from and contribute to.
