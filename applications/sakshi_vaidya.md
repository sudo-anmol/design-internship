## 4. A project you've shipped recently
One project I worked on was improving parts of Snapmint’s checkout and onboarding flow.
What I noticed early on was that users were not necessarily dropping off because the product was confusing overall. It was more about small moments of hesitation. Especially in a fintech context, if something feels even slightly unclear, like pricing or EMI breakdown, users tend to pause or leave.
I spent time going through user behavior and identifying exactly where that hesitation was happening. From there, I reworked parts of the flow by simplifying steps, making the information clearer, especially around pricing, and guiding users better at key decision points instead of overwhelming them.
The end result felt more natural. Users did not have to stop and think as much, and the flow helped them move forward with more confidence.
What I really took away from this was how small UX decisions can have a big impact, especially in trust heavy flows like payments. It made me realize that good design here is not about adding more, but about removing doubt.
