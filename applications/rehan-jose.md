## 4. A project you've shipped recently

A project I’ve shipped recently is Dinely, a food planning and bill-splitting app designed to simplify group dining decisions.
The core problem I focused on was the friction in coordinating plans—people struggling to decide where to eat, tracking orders, and splitting expenses fairly. 
I worked on the end-to-end UX, including defining user flows, designing intuitive interfaces, and creating interaction patterns that make coordination feel seamless rather than chaotic.
One key decision was prioritizing clarity over feature overload, ensuring users could quickly understand who owes what and what the group has decided. 
If I were to iterate further, I would improve the onboarding experience and introduce smarter recommendations based on user preferences to reduce decision fatigue even more.

## 5. Why you want to join Amber

Amber stands out to me because it treats student experiences as a product problem, not just a service. The idea of reducing friction in something as high-stress as student 
accommodation—where decisions are emotional, time-sensitive, and often confusing—is something I find genuinely meaningful.I’m particularly drawn to how Amber operates in a 0→1
