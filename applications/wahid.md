## 4. A project you've shipped recently
**Receipt — Expense Tracking App**
https://wahidui.framer.website/case-studies/receipt

A mobile app designed to make personal expense tracking less tedious. 
I handled the full design — flows, components, visual language. The core 
problem was that most expense apps feel like data entry tools; I tried 
to make logging a receipt feel closer to a reflex than a chore.

What I'd change: I didn't measure anything after shipping. No user testing, 
no data. The design decisions felt right but I can't back them up with 
outcomes — that's something I'm actively fixing in how I approach work now.

## 5. Why you want to join Amber

Student housing is one of those problems that looks boring from the outside 
but is genuinely stressful to live through. The moment a student lands in 
a new city, housing anxiety is real, and most platforms treat it like a 
listings problem when it's actually a trust problem.

What pulls me to Amber specifically is that you're building at the 
intersection of that anxiety and a high-stakes decision — and there's a 
lot of design surface area that can make or break that experience. The 
fact that this role is for an AI product designer suggests you're thinking 
about how AI can actually reduce friction here, not just slap a chatbot on.

I'm self-taught, one year into UX from a graphic design background. 
Most of my work so far has been self-initiated — I'm at the stage where 
I'm actively looking for real constraints to design against. That's part 
of why this matters.
